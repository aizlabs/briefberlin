# A2 German CEFR Guidelines

BriefBerlin A2 articles are for learners who can understand simple everyday German and want to read real topics in controlled language.

## Reader Profile

An A2 learner can usually:

- understand common words and simple phrases;
- follow short texts about familiar topics;
- understand clear statements about time, place, people, and basic reasons;
- use context and glossary help for some new vocabulary.

## Writing Targets

- About 200 words.
- Short paragraphs.
- Mostly main clauses.
- Clear connectors such as `aber`, `denn`, `deshalb`, `auch`, and `danach`.
- Limited passive voice.
- Limited abstract nouns.

## Grammar

Prefer:

- present tense;
- perfect tense for completed events;
- simple modal verbs;
- common separable verbs only when useful;
- direct word order where possible.

Avoid:

- long relative clauses;
- dense noun phrases;
- heavy passive constructions;
- idiomatic expressions;
- unexplained abbreviations.

## Editorial Standard

The text should still feel like a real article, not a grammar exercise. It should preserve factual meaning while making the language accessible.
